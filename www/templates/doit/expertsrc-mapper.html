Answer Questions

Help us integrate Novartis's assay data:

In the panel below, you will be presented pairings of data attributes in an assay schema and attributes in our global schema. These pairs were created because we believe that they store the same type of data.

These attributes are the best matches that were automatically generated by an ensemble of algorithms that compare features of the data attributes. These features include the attribute's name and the data it contains. We need your help to determine whether these matches are correct!


What you're given:

Our Score
A visualization of the composite score for the match is shown for each pair. This is the colored block between the assay attribute and the global attribute.

Other Matches
You can use the dropdown in the matching panel () to view the other possible matches and their attendant scores.

The Data
Use the view source () and match details () buttons to view a sample of the unmapped data and get more infomation about how we generated our scores, respectively.

What you do:

  • You will be given at most 10 questions at a time. If you have been assigned more than 10 questions, more will load automatically when all questions on the page have been answered.
  • Indicate whether you believe the pair is or isn't a match by pressing the and buttons. This may not always be clear, depending on the information that you're given. This is OK - just indicate how sure you are using the confidence slider that appears when you make a decision.
  • You can provide at most one positive answer per question (i.e. the pair is a match!), but as many negative answers as you want. Note that only one answer is needed for us to consider the question "answered".
  • If you change your mind, you can use the button to reset the match to what we originally suggested. Note that after you press "Save" your answer is final!
  • You can navigate back to Expertsrc at any time by pressing the "Done" button.
  • You can view this information at any time by pressing the "Help" button. You can also mouse over the components of the mapping interface to get hints.

Caveats

  • Note that the assay data attributes on the page might not be from the same source. Make sure that you check the name of the source by mousing over the button before you make a decision!

Thanks, and happy mapping!
